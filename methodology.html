How the "most common words" are counted

A short methodology for the arXiv physics.optics and Nature Photonics title quizzes.

1. The title data

  • arXiv physics.optics — every arXiv record whose categories field contains physics.optics (primary or cross-listed), taken from the Kaggle metadata dump (~56,600 titles).
  • Nature Photonics — every article pulled from the Crossref API by ISSN 1749-4893, 2007–2026 (~5,100 titles).
⚠️ The data was taken mid-2026, so 2026 is partial. Only years through 2025 are treated as complete, and the word counts reflect titles as of 2025.

2. Turning a title into words

Each title is broken into topics with one shared pipeline:

  1. Tokenize — lowercase; keep [a-z][a-z\-]+. Hyphens stay inside tokens, so single-photon and non-hermitian remain single words.
  2. Lemmatize — a conservative singularizer maps plurals to one real word (lasers→laser, metasurfaces→metasurface), with a protected set (physics, optics, analysis, …) and an irregulars map (indices→index).
  3. Drop stopwords — generic English and scientific-prose filler (using, novel, study, system, …) are removed.
  4. n-grams — adjacent uni-, bi-, and tri-grams are formed so multi-word concepts (frequency comb, neural network, perovskite solar cell) count as a unit.

3. Merging equivalent phrases

Each n-gram is reduced to a canonical key = the sorted set of its constituent words. This makes counting independent of word order and hyphenation:

  • machine-learning and machine learning → merged.
  • photonic integrated and integrated photonic → merged.

For display, the most common surface form actually seen is shown. A few frequent-but-uninformative words (optical, light, …) are manually excluded.

4. Counting

  • Document frequency — each topic is counted once per title (presence, not raw frequency).
  • The quiz lists the 20 most frequent topics in each dataset, measured over titles through the most recent complete year (2025).

5. Known limitations

  • The tokenizer is letters-only, so non-ASCII and numerals are stripped (MoS₂→mos, moiré→moir).
  • Lemmatization is rule-based, not a full morphological analyzer, so a few forms split or merge imperfectly.
  • 2026 is partial and excluded from the counts.
